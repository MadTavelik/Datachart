10 Years After Sandy, Flooding Resiliency Efforts in Rockaways Show Bias.

While the boardwalk and ocean side of the penisula see investments, Far Rockaway and Edgemere continue to suffer the most flooding with 0 construction efforts.

The Bayswater neighborhood on the bayside of the Rockaways has seen no improvements despite long-standing requests for a new seawall by the community. Photo By Christopher Janaro

By Chris Janaro

November 18, 2022

On the tenth anniversary of Hurricane Sandy, politicians such as NY State Governor Kathy Hochul gave praise to the resiliency efforts made in the Rockaways and lauded the over $45 million investment in community reconstruction along the boardwalk and more affluent southern ocean side of the isolated peninsula however flooding data collected in the area over the last decade has shown that the Jaimaca Bayside neighborhoods of Far Rockaway and Edgemere continue to suffer the most flooding year over year with no construction efforts to be even started yet.

Col. Matthew Luzzatto, commander of the NY District Army Corp of Engineers, said, "We are looking at potentially 2025; we'll see the first contract of actual execution on the bayside"

Queens Community Board 14 has consistently cited bayside seawall repair and drainage as a top priority. It has remained listed at the top of their Capital Expense Budget Request Reports to the city for decades, even before Sandy hit.
